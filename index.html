01 30-Second Pulse Billing

Closing the pricing gap with competitors
Live
“
What the market told us

Prospects were walking away because competitors billed in 30-second blocks while we billed in 60s only. A 35-second reminder call cost a full minute on Mio — but 30s elsewhere.

"Your competitor charges us for 30 seconds. Why are we paying for a full minute?"
What we shipped & what to tell customers

Configurable pulse per account — 30s or 60s. Bring it up when:

  • Customer runs short-form calls (reminders, quick nudges, reactivation).
  • Customer is comparing METS cost to a competitor.
  • Renewal or pricing pushback — pulse becomes a commercial lever.
Stops deal losses on commercials · protects renewals

Call Concurrency

"Why can't I dial faster?" — what to say
📞
The one-liner

Dedicated lines = parallel calls at a moment. Concurrency defines how many calls a client can place simultaneously when a campaign runs. We allocate dedicated channels per entity — locked in the MoU, never shared.

How it works
1
MoU-Based Allocation
Each client's concurrency is set in the MoU — the max calls flowing simultaneously during a campaign.
2
Dedicated per Entity
Channels are reserved for the client — never shared. Guaranteed parallel capacity at all times.
3
Upgrade on Demand
Clients can buy additional channels to finish jobs faster. CS upsell lever when high volume is seen.

Per channel / 9 hrs: 9 × 60 = 540 min ÷ 1 min/call = 540 calls

Important caveat — set this expectation with the customer

This assumes continuous calling, no idle time, no agent delays, no vendor downtime. Real-world numbers are lower. For high-volume clients, recommend more concurrent channels upfront to avoid queue buildup.

How We QC Mio Voice

The process — and where CS plugs in
👥
The QC Team

A dedicated 3-member QC team inside Mio Voice runs random sampling of calls across all active clients — to catch defects early, maintain consistent quality, and surface client-impacting issues before they escalate.

End-to-end QC workflow
1
Random Sampling
Pull random calls across all active clients
2
Call Review
QC reviews & categorises quality issues
3
Spam Check
Verify if calling number is flagged as SPAM
4
CS Escalation
Email concerned CS POC if spam found
5
Number Rotation
Replace number post client approval & payment
Steps 1–2 · Sampling & Review
QC manually reviews each sampled call for agent performance, TTS pronunciation, STT accuracy, system detection issues, technical/network problems, and overall conversation quality. All findings are logged in the QC dashboard with category tags.
Step 3 · Spam Number Verification
For every sampled call, QC checks if the outbound number is flagged as SPAM by carriers / Truecaller. Spam-flagged numbers kill pickup rates and damage client experience — early detection is critical.
Steps 4–5 · CS coordination & number rotation
When a number is spam-flagged, QC emails the concerned CS POC with affected client details. CS drives the client conversation: (a) get approval for replacement, (b) inform the client that payment is required for rotation, (c) execute the rotation once client confirms.

Customer Expectations · 03 · For CS

Customer Expectation

Common questions & expectations your customers may raise
Agent Behaviour
No Memory · Inbound = Unknown Caller
Mio agents (OB & IB) don't carry memory across calls. For inbound specifically, no name / course / past context is fetched — every inbound is treated as an unknown caller.
Don't promise personalised inbound conversations. Set this expectation upfront.
Agent Behaviour
Inbound = Callback, NOT Website Redirect
The "inbound agent" is a callback agent for a paired outbound. It is not a website call-routing or call-deflection agent.
Clarify this if a client asks about routing inbound calls from their website.
Multilingual
Slightly Higher Response Time
Multilingual agents respond slightly slower than English-only or Hindi-only agents. This is expected behaviour, not a defect.
Flag this to the customer at launch. Don't treat as a bug.
Multilingual
Max 2 Languages per Agent
A multilingual agent supports a maximum of 2 languages — typically English + one regional (Hindi / Tamil / Telugu / etc.).
For 3+ languages, the customer needs separate agents per language pair.
Setup · CS Owned
Multilingual SPOC at Testing
CS / Sales must arrange a multilingual SPOC from the client side during agent creation, alteration, and testing phases.
Owned by CS Owner or Sales Owner — pre-flag this during onboarding.
Setup · DID Pairing
Shared DID → Paired Callbacks
If 2+ outbound agents share a DID, each outbound gets its own paired callback agent. One callback agent cannot cover multiple outbound agents on the same DID.
Important during setup — affects callback agent count & configuration.
